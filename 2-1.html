ROK Country Overview

Country Profile: Republic of Korea

Before signing of the K-CA FTA and the Republic of Korea(ROK)’s incorporation into CABEI, ROK was just one of many external players in the global market rather than a partner for the Central America(CA) region. Considering the physical distance of 13,558km between the Republic of Korea (ROK) and Central America, it is natural that ROK is unfamiliar to the region.

With the K-CA FTA fully effective and ROK’s increasing presences in the region, now is the time to learn about the country and potential opportunities in the ROK market.

  • Country Name : Republic of Korea
  • Capital City : Seoul
  • Population : 51.63 million (as of 2022)
  • GDP : USD 1,664.3 billion (as of 2022)
  • Per Capita GNI : USD 33,592 (as of 2022)
  • GDP Growth Rate : 2.6% (as of 2022)

  • National Language : Korean
  • National Flag : Taegeukgi
  • National Flower : Mugunghwa (Rose of Sharon)

Economic Fundamentals

The Republic of Korea is one of the few countries that has successfully transformed itself from a low-income to a high-income economy and a global leader in innovation and technology. The country has achieved remarkable success in recent decades in combining rapid economic growth with significant poverty reduction, with real gross domestic product (GDP) growing on average by 5.7% annually between 1980 and 2022. It is the 10th largest GDP in the World. The Republic of Korea’s gross national income (GNI) per capita increased rapidly from US$67 in the early 1950s to US$33,592 in 2022.

Against this backdrop, the Republic of Korea was the first former aid recipient to become a member of the Development Assistance Committee (DAC) of the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) in 2010. As of 2024, the Republic of Korea is a donor member of multiple multilateral development banks (MDBs) including the World Bank Group, the Asian Development Bank (ADB), the Inter-American Development Bank (IADB), the Central American Bank for Economic Integration (CABEI), and others. In addition, the Republic of Korea's strong economic stability is reflected in its sovereign credit rating, which recorded Aa2 (stable) by Moody’s as of 2024. It is an affirmation of robust financial health and a promising investment landscape.

Trade Status

This remarkable economic stature reflects its innovative industries and robust global commerce, positioning ROK as a leading nation shaping the future of the global market. As of 2023, ROK is the 6th largest trading country in the world with a total of 21 FTAs in effect with 59 countries. About 85% of the total trade is facilitated with the countries that have FTA with ROK.

Republic of Korea’s FTA Partners 2024

* Source : FTA Korea

The largest trade partners of ROK have been the People’s Republic of China, the United States of America, and Vietnam, which accounted for over 43% of the total export to the world and over 37% of the total import from the world as of 2022.

When it comes to ROK’s trade with Central America, the trade value for both imports and exports is small accounting for about 0.20% and 0.41% of the total trade value in 2022. Despite the comparably small in size, both imports and exports increased from 2020 to 2022 with further increase expected along with the impacts of the K-CA FTA signed.
