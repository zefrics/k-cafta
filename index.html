Success Story

The stories of remarkable exporters and importers to assist Central American (CA) businesses in paving the way to success in the ROK market.

The K-CAFTA online platform is the initiative aiming to bolster trade and investment
between the Republic of Korea (ROK) and Central American (CA) countries
by providing a centralized hub for trade information and capacity building.

The Platform is the 1st Central America-focused online platform dedicated to promoting
business between ROK and CA under Republic of Korea – Central America FTA (K-CA FTA).

It seeks to help CA businesses facilitate and expedite their exportation towards ROK
by addressing information asymmetry and enhancing stakeholders’ access
to ROK market information.

In addition, under the K-CA FTA, it targets to contribute to the diversification and
expansion of CA’s economy, as well as strengthening partnerships with ROK businesses,
and fostering sustainable economic development of both regions.

Sponsed by

The Central American Bank for Economic Integration (CABEI), is an international multilateral development financial institution.
Its resources are continuously invested in projects that foster development to reduce poverty and inequality; strengthen regional integration and the competitive insertion of its member countries in the global economy; providing special attention to environmental sustainability.

Operated by

The Chamber of Commerce Korea-LAC (CAMCOR) is the host and operator of the platform in partnership with CABEI.
Based on the expertise of trade facilitation and business advisory services for CA firms, as well as relationships with Korean market players from both public and private sector the host ensures the continuation of the provision of service and information of the online platform.

Partners

To support and promote the trade facilitation between the Republic of Korea and the Central American region, the online platform is collaborating with multiple partners that have knowledge, networks, and resources for business transactions in both ROK and CA.

To encourage expansion and diversification of trade between ROK and CA countries,
the first official FTA negotiation was kicked off in June 2015 and the final agreement
was reached in November 2016 after seven rounds of negotiation meetings.

As a result, in February 2018, ROK signed FTA with five Central American countries
: Costa Rica, El Salvador Honduras, Nicaragua, and Panama,
and ROK became the first East Asian country to sign a joint FTA with these five economies.

In case of Guatemala, an initial participant of the K-CA FTA negotiation
from the initial stage in 2015, the negotiation was concluded
and the FTA with ROK was signed in September 2023.

While the ratification of the FTA between ROK and Guatemala is pending as of May 2024,
an official letter of Guatemala’s intent for the accession to the K-CA FTA agreement
was sent to ROK in January 2024.

The effective years of the FTA with the five Central American countries,
excluding Guatemala, are as follows: Nicaragua and Honduras in October 2019,
Costa Rica in November 2019, El Salvador in January 2020, and Panama in March 2021.
The effective year of Korea-Guatemala FTA is expected to be 2024.

Economic Fundamentals

The Republic of Korea is one of the few countries that has successfully transformed itself from a low-income to a high-income economy and a global leader in innovation and technology. The country has achieved remarkable success in recent decades in combining rapid economic growth with significant poverty reduction, with real gross domestic product (GDP) growing on average by 5.7% annually between 1980 and 2022. It is the 10th largest GDP in the World. The Republic of Korea’s gross national income (GNI) per capita increased rapidly from US$67 in the early 1950s to US$33,592 in 2022.

Against this backdrop, the Republic of Korea was the first former aid recipient to become a member of the Development Assistance Committee (DAC) of the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) in 2010. As of 2024, the Republic of Korea is a donor member of multiple multilateral development banks (MDBs) including the World Bank Group, the Asian Development Bank (ADB), the Inter-American Development Bank (IADB), the Central American Bank for Economic Integration (CABEI), and others. In addition, the Republic of Korea's strong economic stability is reflected in its sovereign credit rating, which recorded Aa2 (stable) by Moody’s as of 2024. It is an affirmation of robust financial health and a promising investment landscape.

Trade Status

This remarkable economic stature reflects its innovative industries and robust global commerce, positioning the Republic of Korea as a leading nation shaping the future of the global market.

As of 2023, the Republic of Korea is the 6th largest trading country in the world with a total of 21 FTAs in effect with 59 countries. About 85% of the total trade is facilitated with the countries that have FTA with the Republic of Korea.

The largest trade partners of the Republic of Korea have been the People’s Republic of China, the United States of America, and Vietnam, which accounted for over 43% of the total export to the world and over 37% of the total import from the world as of 2022.

When it comes to the Republic of Korea’s trade with the Central American region, the trade value for both imports and exports is small accounting for about 0.20% and 0.41% of the total trade value in 2022. Despite the comparably small in size, both imports and exports increased from 2020 to 2022 with further increase expected along with the impacts of the FTA signed between the Republic of Korea and Central America.

Busan Port is the world's 2nd busiest transshipment port and the world's No.7 container port, handling 76.6% of domestic container cargo as of 2022.
As such, Busan port is the largest seaport in Korea, and a global transshipment hub. It is a super port recording 21.91 million TEU of annual container throughput in 2022, and a Northeast Asia logistics hub connected with 500 ports around 150 countries through operation of over 179 weekly voyages.

Considering the largest cargo service capacity, as well as its convenient location to reach from the Americas, the marine transport route and cost estimate between CA and South Korea will be based on setting Busan Port as the destination.

The ocean transportation routes from Central America to Korea typically follow similar paths, with major ports requiring transshipments in locations such as Mexico or Panama. A direct shipping option is not available, except for Panama.

Shipping companies and routes from Central American countries to South Korea are shown in the Figure below. In addition, when shipping from the Americas to the (north)east Asia, it is usual to arrive at Yokohama, Japan first, and then to the destinations in South Korea and PR China.

Shipping Route

Overall, it takes at least 2 ports and minimum 29 days to maximum 62 days to ship freight from CA ports to Busan port, Korea.

Shipping Cost Estimate

The standard shipping cost for a 40' General Purpose container (GP) is minimum 643 USD and maximum 2,841 USD, showing that there is a large difference in transportation costs depending on the country and carrier. Also, a 40’ Reefer container costs minimum 4,145 USD and maximum 5,595 USD and there are some routes and carriers in certain country where reefer containers are not available.

LATIDA stands for 'La Tierra Prometida,' which embodies the vision of creating a sustainable trading ecosystem that benefits all stakeholders. LATIDA aims to bridge the two distant markets and lead to success by well managing and mitigating the risks of long distance and cultural differences. With a commitment to more than mere transactions, LATIDA empowers exporters with market insights and years of sales data, fostering a collaborative approach that ensures mutual success. Since its founding in 2021, LATIDA has orchestrated partnerships with major Korean players like Starbucks, Coupang, and Baemin, revolutionizing the import landscape with quality products sourced from across the globe, with a special focus on Latin America.
