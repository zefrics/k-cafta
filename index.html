Initative and Objectives

The K-CAFTA online platform is the initiative aiming to bolster trade and investment
between the Republic of Korea (ROK) and Central American (CA) countries
by providing a centralized hub for trade information and capacity building.

The Platform is the 1st Central America-focused online platform dedicated to promoting
business between ROK and CA under Republic of Korea – Central America FTA (K-CA FTA).

It seeks to help CA businesses facilitate and expedite their exportation towards ROK
by addressing information asymmetry and enhancing stakeholders’ access
to ROK market information.

In addition, under the K-CA FTA, it targets to contribute to the diversification and
expansion of CA’s economy, as well as strengthening partnerships with ROK businesses,
and fostering sustainable economic development of both regions.

Sponsed by

The Central American Bank for Economic Integration (CABEI), is an international multilateral development financial institution. Its resources are continuously invested in projects that foster development to reduce poverty and inequality; strengthen regional integration and the competitive insertion of its member countries in the global economy; providing special attention to environmental sustainability.

Operated by

The Chamber of Commerce Korea-LAC (CAMCOR) is the host and operator of the platform in partnership with CABEI. Based on the expertise of trade facilitation and business advisory services for CA firms, as well as relationships with Korean market players from both public and private sector the host ensures the continuation of the provision of service and information of the online platform..

Partners

To support and promote the trade facilitation between the Republic of Korea and the Central American region, the online platform is collaborating with multiple partners that have knowledge, networks, and resources for business transactions in both ROK and CA.

Initative and Objectives

To encourage expansion and diversification of trade between ROK and CA countries,
the first official FTA negotiation was kicked off in June 2015 and the final agreement
was reached in November 2016 after seven rounds of negotiation meetings.

As a result, in February 2018, ROK signed FTA with five Central American countries
: Costa Rica, El Salvador Honduras, Nicaragua, and Panama,
and ROK became the first East Asian country to sign a joint FTA with these five economies.

In case of Guatemala, an initial participant of the K-CA FTA negotiation
from the initial stage in 2015, the negotiation was concluded
and the FTA with ROK was signed in September 2023.

While the ratification of the FTA between ROK and Guatemala is pending as of May 2024,
an official letter of Guatemala’s intent for the accession to the K-CA FTA agreement
was sent to ROK in January 2024.

The effective years of the FTA with the five Central American countries,
excluding Guatemala, are as follows: Nicaragua and Honduras in October 2019,
Costa Rica in November 2019, El Salvador in January 2020, and Panama in March 2021.
The effective year of Korea-Guatemala FTA is expected to be 2024.
